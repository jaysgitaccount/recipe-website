Chicken Rice

Description

This recipe was given to me by a friend of mine. It is not Hainanese Chicken Rice, but rather a pared-down version of that dish that is quick and convenient to make for dinner. I make this a lot when I want to pretend I'm eating something special but I don't want to actually put in the work to make anything elaborate.
My favourite part of this dish is the sauce that you dip your chicken pieces into. It is addictively salty and has a great mouth feel. The soup is also highly customisable, so you can make it as hearty as you want.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken drumsticks, room temp
  • 2 stalks spring onion trimmed, 1/4 stalk finely chopped
  • 2 slices of ginger for soup, 2 tsp minced ginger for sauce
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped ginger and spring onion, mixed in a 1:1 ratio
  • 1/4 cup non-fragrant cooking oil

Method

Chicken and Soup

  1. Start cooking rice if you haven't already.
  2. Fill a large pot (big enough for all the chicken) with cold water and some salt.
  3. Trim the ends of spring onions, cut into large sections and add to pot.
  4. Turn on the heat and bring to a boil.
  5. Once the water boils, turn off the heat and cover to let it cook the chicken nicely. It will be done in 20 minutes.
  6. When chicken is cooked, remove from the pot and set aside. Remove spring onion bits and discard.
  7. Add more salt, ginger, chopped spring onion and any other desired ingredients to the soup. Boil further for flavour.

Sauce

  1. Mix minced ginger and spring onion in a small heat-resistant bowl with 1.5 tsp salt.
  2. Heat up the oil over the stove. When a wooden chopstick dipped into the pan produces gentle but steady bubbles, it's at the right temperature.
  3. Carefully pour the hot oil over the ginger and spring onion to cook it briefly. Then mix and serve with rice, chicken and soup.
